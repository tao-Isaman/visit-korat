## Welcome to Korat!

Nakhon Ratchasima — or **Korat** as everyone calls it — is Thailand's largest province by area and the gateway to the Isan (northeastern) region. While most tourists head straight for Bangkok, Chiang Mai, or the islands, Korat offers an authentic Thai experience with far fewer crowds.

## Why Visit Korat?

- **Rich history** — Ancient Khmer ruins at Phimai that predate Angkor Wat
- **Incredible nature** — Khao Yai National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site
- **Amazing food** — From the signature Pad Mee Korat to the best Isan cuisine
- **Authentic culture** — Experience real Thai life without the tourist bubble
- **Great value** — Much more affordable than tourist hotspots

## Top 5 Must-Do Experiences

1. **Explore Phimai Historical Park** — Walk through 1,000-year-old Khmer ruins
2. **Visit Khao Yai National Park** — Spot wild elephants and chase waterfalls
3. **Eat Pad Mee Korat** — Try the city's signature noodle dish
4. **Pay respects at Thao Suranari Monument** — Understand Korat's spirit
5. **Browse Save One Night Market** — Shop and eat like a local

## Best Time to Visit

- **November to February** — Cool season, ideal weather (20-30°C)
- **March to May** — Hot season, 35-40°C but fewer visitors
- **June to October** — Rainy season, great for waterfalls and lush scenery

## How to Get Here

- **From Bangkok by car** — 2.5-3 hours via Highway 2 (Mittraphap)
- **By bus** — Frequent departures from Mo Chit (3-4 hours, 200-350 THB)
- **By train** — Scenic route from Hua Lamphong (4-5 hours, 100-1,300 THB)
- **By air** — Nakhon Ratchasima Airport (limited service), or fly to Don Mueang/Suvarnabhumi and take ground transport

## Essential Tips

- Learn basic Thai phrases — English is less common here than in tourist areas
- Carry cash — many local vendors don't accept cards
- Download Grab — it works in Korat for taxis and food delivery
- Respect temple etiquette — cover shoulders and knees
- Try everything — the food is the highlight!
