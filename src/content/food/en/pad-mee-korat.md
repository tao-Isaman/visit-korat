## What is Pad Mee Korat?

Pad Mee Korat (ผัดหมี่โคราช) is the city's most iconic dish and a source of immense local pride. Unlike Pad Thai, which is sweeter and found nationwide, Pad Mee Korat has a distinctive flavor that combines:

- **Sweet** from palm sugar
- **Sour** from tamarind paste
- **Savory** from fermented soybean paste
- **Spicy** from dried chilies

The thin rice noodles are stir-fried with tofu, shrimp or pork, bean sprouts, Chinese chives, and a special sauce recipe that varies from cook to cook. The result is a deeper, more complex flavor than Pad Thai.

## Where to Try It

The best Pad Mee Korat is found at:

1. **Street vendors near Thao Suranari Monument** — Authentic and cheap (30-50 THB)
2. **Save One Market** — Multiple stalls competing to be the best
3. **Local restaurants around Jomsurangyat Road** — Sit-down experience with variations

## How to Eat It

- Squeeze fresh lime over the top
- Add crushed peanuts and dried chili flakes to taste
- Try the "wrapped in egg" version (ห่อไข่) for a richer experience
- Pair with a cold Cha Yen (Thai iced tea)
