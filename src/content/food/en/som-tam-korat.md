## Isan Food in Korat

Korat sits at the crossroads between central Thailand and the Isan region, giving it a unique culinary position. Here you'll find Isan flavors that are authentic yet slightly milder than the far northeast — perfect for adventurous eaters.

## Essential Dishes

### Som Tam (Papaya Salad)
- **Som Tam Thai** — The "safe" version with peanuts and dried shrimp
- **Som Tam Poo Pla Ra** — The real deal with fermented fish and salted crab (for the brave!)
- **Som Tam Korat** — Local variation with unique seasoning

### Grilled & Meat Dishes
- **Gai Yang** — Isan-style grilled chicken, marinated and smoky
- **Larb Moo** — Spicy minced pork salad with roasted rice powder
- **Nam Tok Moo** — Grilled pork salad with herbs and chili
- **Sai Krok Isan** — Fermented pork sausages, tangy and delicious

### Always With Sticky Rice
Every Isan meal comes with **khao niao** (sticky rice) served in small bamboo baskets. Use your hands to pinch off a ball and scoop up the dishes.

## Where to Eat

- Any local market or food stall — look for the busiest ones
- Night markets throughout the city
- Roadside restaurants along Mittraphap Highway
