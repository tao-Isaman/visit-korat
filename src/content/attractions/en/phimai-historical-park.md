## About Phimai Historical Park

Phimai Historical Park is one of the most significant Khmer temple complexes in Thailand. The sanctuary was originally built during the reign of Khmer King Jayavarman V in the late 10th to early 11th century, making parts of it older than Angkor Wat in Cambodia.

The main sanctuary, Prasat Hin Phimai, is oriented to the southeast — directly toward Angkor — connected by an ancient Khmer highway. The temple complex features stunning sandstone carvings depicting scenes from Hindu mythology and Buddhist teachings.

## Highlights

- **Prasat Hin Phimai** — The main tower standing 28 meters tall with intricate sandstone carvings
- **Pratu Chai** — The impressive victory gate at the southern entrance
- **Phimai National Museum** — Located nearby, housing artifacts found in the area
- **The Ancient Banyan Tree** — Thailand's largest banyan tree, located just outside the park

## Tips

- Visit early morning to avoid the heat and crowds
- Hire a local guide at the entrance for deeper historical context (around 300-500 THB)
- Combine with a visit to Sai Ngam (the banyan tree grove) nearby
- The annual Phimai Festival in November features sound and light shows at the ruins
