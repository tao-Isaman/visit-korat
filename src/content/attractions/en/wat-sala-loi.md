## About Wat Sala Loi

Wat Sala Loi is one of Korat's most visually distinctive temples. Its main hall (ubosot) is uniquely designed in the shape of a Chinese junk boat, a creative fusion of modern architecture with traditional Buddhist temple design.

The temple was built in the Ayutthaya period but underwent a dramatic renovation in 1967-1973, when the famous Thai architect Ajarn Wichit Wathakan redesigned the main hall in its current boat-like form. The result won an award from the Association of Siamese Architects.

## Highlights

- **Boat-shaped Ubosot** — The main prayer hall with its striking ship design
- **Interior Murals** — Beautiful modern murals depicting Buddhist stories
- **Serene Grounds** — Peaceful temple gardens along the Lam Takhong River
- **Dan Kwian Pottery** — Decorative elements featuring famous local pottery

## Tips

- Located near the river, it's a pleasant walk from the city center
- Dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees) as this is an active temple
- Best photographed in the golden hour light
