## About Khao Yai National Park

Established in 1962, Khao Yai is Thailand's first and one of its most beloved national parks. Covering over 2,168 square kilometers across four provinces, it was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2005 as part of the Dong Phayayen-Khao Yai Forest Complex.

The park is home to over 3,000 plant species, 320 bird species, and diverse wildlife including wild elephants, gibbons, hornbills, and even some elusive clouded leopards.

## Must-See Spots

- **Haew Narok Waterfall** — The park's tallest waterfall at 150 meters, the most dramatic during rainy season
- **Haew Suwat Waterfall** — Famous from the movie "The Beach," a beautiful 20-meter cascade
- **Mo Singto Reservoir** — Great spot for wildlife watching, especially wild elephants at dusk
- **Pha Diew Dai Viewpoint** — Stunning cliff-edge viewpoint overlooking the valley
- **Bat Cave** — Witness millions of wrinkle-lipped bats emerge at sunset

## Tips

- Hire a guide for night safari tours to spot wildlife
- Bring warm clothing — temperatures can drop to 10°C in cool season
- The best time for waterfall viewing is July to November (rainy season)
- Leeches are common during wet season — bring salt or leech socks
