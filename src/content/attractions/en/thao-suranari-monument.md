## About Thao Suranari Monument

Standing proudly at the western gate of Korat's old city, the Thao Suranari Monument (อนุสาวรีย์ท้าวสุรนารี) honors Khunying Mo, the wife of a local deputy governor who led the resistance against Lao invaders in 1826.

The bronze statue depicts Lady Mo in traditional dress, holding a sword. It is the most revered landmark in Korat and a symbol of the city's brave spirit. Locals come daily to pay respects with offerings of flowers and incense.

## The Thao Suranari Festival

Every year from March 23 to April 3, the city holds a grand festival in her honor. It features:

- Traditional performances and parades
- Local food markets and craft fairs
- Light and sound shows telling her story
- Paying respects ceremonies

## Tips

- The area around the monument is lively at night with food vendors
- A great starting point for exploring the old city
- Visit the adjacent Chumphon Gate, part of the old city wall
